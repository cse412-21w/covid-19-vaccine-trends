COVID-19 TRENDS

an exploration of global vaccine distribution

Countries around the world have started vaccinating against COVID-19, signalling an approaching end to the pandemic. However, there is still a lot of ambiguity around timelines on when vaccines will be available or when certain groups will be eligible to receive them. Most states and countries are currently operating on a very strict rollout plan in structured phases to get the vaccine to vulnerable populations first. By analyzing both global and national vaccination data to visualize trends and estimate when we can hit certain milestones as well as look at country specific data and compare how vaccine distribution plans differ across the globe.

A look at the global vaccine rollout

The first COVID-19 vaccine was administered the first week of December in 2020, marking the start of the end of the pandemic. Since then, multiple vaccines have been developed and approved for use around the world. Vaccine rollout plans differ greatly, here's where countries around the world line up in terms of what percent of their populations have been vaccinated

There are currently multiple approved vaccines, some relying on a two-dose strategy while others only require one shot. For the purpose of this next visualization, anyone who has received even one shot (out of a possible two) is counted in the country's vaccination numbers. For example, if a country is labeled at 50%, half of their population has received at least one shot even if that means some residents still need a second. The data reflected below is accurate as of March 3th 2021.

A look at the national vaccine rollout

For a more in depth view of the vaccine rollout plan, we can look at the daily vaccination rate. In the United States, most states plan on moving in phases to decide who is eligible to get the vaccine. In Phase 1, frontline emergency responders and health care workers who were patient facing were able to get vaccinated. As states continue to move through phases, more and more people become eligible. There are currently three FDA approved vaccines in the United States as of March 15 2021 - the Pfizer vaccine, the Moderna vaccine, and most recently, the Johnson & Johnson vaccine. In a recent statement, President Biden announced that all Americans will be eligible for vaccinations by May 1.

The United States has had some of the most serious Covid-19 outbreaks from any country. In order to combat this, serious effort to administer vaccines has been made.
